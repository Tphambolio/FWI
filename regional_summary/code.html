Fire Weather Index — Danger Rating

The FWI is a unitless index combining fuel moisture and wind. The left half of each map marker shows the danger class.

Class FWI Range Meaning
Low0 – 8Fuels moist, spread unlikely
Moderate9 – 17Some fire activity possible
High18 – 32Rapid spread, control difficult
Very High33 – 49Intense fire, suppression limited
Extreme50+Crown fire conditions, evacuate
Head Fire Intensity — Byram Class

HFI (kW/m) is the energy output at the fire front from FBP. The right half of each marker shows the intensity class. Click any marker for the kW/m value.

Class HFI (kW/m) Meaning
1-Low< 10Direct attack with hand tools · Should anchor
2-Mod10 – 499Direct attack with hand tools · Should anchor
3-High500 – 1 999Direct attack with pump and hose · Should anchor
4-VH2 000 – 3 999Indirect attack · Direct on less intense area · Must anchor
5-Ext4 000 – 9 999Indirect attack · Direct on less intense area · Must anchor
6-Cat≥ 10 000No direct attack — evacuate structure zone
